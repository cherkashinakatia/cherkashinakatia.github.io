Body care

Before splurging on expensive products for your skin, make sure that you try a few simple and natural life hacks that make a huge difference in your skin care.

For many people, the cold clear days of winter bring more than just a rosy glow to the cheeks. They also bring uncomfortable dryness to the skin of the face, hands, and feet. For some people, the problem is worse than just a general tight, dry feeling: They get skin so dry it results in flaking, cracking, even eczema (in which the skin becomes inflamed).

Winter is a lovely respite from the hot sun. But it does come with its share of trouble. If you have flawless complexion, then, perhaps, you might escape from the evil clutches of winter. People with oily or dry skin aren’t that lucky.

But it doesn’t mean you go through winter with bad skin. Here are a few tips for glowing skin in winter. Don’t worry, the winter face care tips are not complicated. In fact, they are every day actions that will help your skin face the season with courage.



Cleansing
Wash your face 2-3 times a day and deep cleanse at night before going to bed. Use cold milk and cotton to fight dryness.


Scrubbing
Scrubbing should be an integral part of your beauty routine. In winter, however, limit scrubbing to once or twice a week and use a mild product.

Your skin secretes more oil in winter to compensate for the dryness. Thus, winter care for oily skin means more diligent toning in winter.

You still need to moisturise your skin daily. Instead of a thick cold cream, you can use almond or extra virgin olive oil to moisturise without blocking pores. 

Winter care for dry skin means applying homemade hydrating masks to lock in moisture. Use readily available ingredients like honey, buttermilk, avocado, yogurt, olive and jojoba oils, almond oil, bananas and aloe.


Dry skin loses a lot of moisture. Keep drinking water regularly to keep your body and skin hydrated.





The sun might seem weak, but you still need protection from it. Don’t skip the sunscreen when you step out.

Luxuriating under a hot shower might seemappealing, but in actuality you are drying your skin if the temperature is too high, and if you stay in for too long.

  • Choose the right moisturiser

Switch to an oil-based moisturiser and stick to one with natural ingredients.

Besides the sunscreen, you must cover all exposed skin when out in the cold to protect your skin from the cold biting wind.

Use a deep-conditioning night cream on your hands and feet when you go to bed. You can also wear gloves to keep the moisture locked in.
